# Copy Frameworks

## Headline Formulas

| Formula | Example | Best For |
|---------|---------|----------|
| {Outcome} without {Pain} | "Launch faster without drowning in config" | Problem/solution |
| The {Category} for {Audience} | "The CRM for solo consultants" | Audience-specific |
| Never {Bad Thing} again | "Never lose a lead to a messy inbox again" | Pain avoidance |
| {Number} {Things} that {Outcome} | "7 automations that save 10 hours/week" | Listicles |
| How {Authority} {Achieves Outcome} | "How Stripe processes $500B/year" | Case study |
| {Tool} vs {Tool}: {Verdict} | "Notion vs Obsidian: Not even close" | Comparison |
| Why {Common Belief} is Wrong | "Why 'build it and they'll come' is killing your startup" | Contrarian |
| {Outcome} in {Timeframe} | "Set up CI/CD in 15 minutes" | How-to/tutorial |

## Section Templates

### Above the Fold
```
Headline → Subheadline → Primary CTA
[Social proof: logos or stats]
```

### Problem Section
```
H2: [Pain point as question or statement]
- Describe the specific pain (mirror customer language)
- Show consequences of inaction
- Transition to solution
```

### Solution Section
```
H2: [How {Product} solves this]
- Connect feature to benefit to outcome
- Show use cases
- Prove it works (stats, testimonials)
```

### How It Works
```
H2: How it works
Step 1: [Action] → [Result]
Step 2: [Action] → [Result]
Step 3: [Action] → [Result]
[CTA: "See it in action"]
```

### Objection Handling
```
H2: "But what about...?"
- Address top 3 objections
- Risk reversal (guarantee, free trial, no credit card)
- FAQ accordion
```

### Final CTA
```
H2: [Outcome-focused statement]
[Supporting text: 1-2 lines]
[Strong CTA button]
[Risk reversal under button]
```

## CTA Copy Rules

**Weak (avoid):** Submit, Sign Up, Learn More, Click Here, Get Started
**Strong (use):** Start Free Trial, Get [Specific Thing], See [Product] in Action, Create Your First [Thing]

**Formula:** [Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]

## Natural Transitions

Between sections, use:
- **Question transition**: "So how do you actually do this?"
- **Consequence transition**: "That's the problem. Here's the fix."
- **Preview transition**: "Let's break this down step by step."
- **Contrast transition**: "Most teams do X. The best teams do Y."
- **Story transition**: "When [person] ran into this problem..."

Avoid: "Now let's discuss," "Moving on to," "It's important to note"
